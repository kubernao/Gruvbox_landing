The same memo layout, year after year

This screen made sense when work lived on paper and you "saved" to a floppy disk. It still runs — but it was never rebuilt for fast drafts, links, or writing with help from a model. It is an old tool that many teams keep open out of habit, not because it still fits the job.

Newer ways to write are here. This one is familiar, slow to change, and quietly on the way out.

This doesn't make writers happy.

The old word processor: familiar, heavy, and still shaped for print-era memos. It wasn't built for how drafts move today, and nobody feels happy working in it.

Here is what our solution looks like:

Design goals

Optimized for writer happiness

We tune layout and type for long sessions—and keep every draft findable, not buried in duplicate files.

Version history

Version history that gives you time-travelling super-powers.

AI enables faster writing than ever before—but it also allows for faster and bigger mistakes. That's why our editor has a Big Ass Undo Button™ built in. Make big mistakes, experiment—and keep the good while throwing out the bad.

Cherry-pick AI changes

Format

Mermaid & LaTeX — first-class ideas

Diagrams and math live in the doc—not pasted images—so they stay editable and AI-friendly as you extend the project.

Editor

Gruvbox warmth, on purpose

Low-contrast Gruvbox colors for long sessions—same care in layout and how often we interrupt you.

Assistant

You steer what the model sees, says, and changes.

Set guardrails once—style, house rules, characters—and the model stays inside them. You choose what it reads, what it writes, and what actually lands in the file.

Rules, memory, and your file

Control the input, the output, and what lands on the page

Attach style docs, rules (POV, tone, show-don't-tell), and character sheets—ground truth you don't retype every prompt. Asks and answers stay inside the lines you drew.

Memory holds project context; commands drive the assistant; it can read the files you allow—grounded in your tree, not a stale paste. When it edits the open doc, every change is highlighted: accept, reject, or revert by line or batch. No silent rewrites.
